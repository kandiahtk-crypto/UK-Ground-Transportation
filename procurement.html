Supplier positioning

Secondary and regional delivery partner

We support established travel organisers as a structured secondary or regional partner, providing capacity resilience, route-feasibility review and disciplined touring execution where timings, access and driver-hours compliance matter.

Typical use cases

  • Overflow and peak-season capacity support
  • Regional touring blocks and contracted allocations
  • Gateway-linked touring programmes including air and cruise
  • Long-distance England–Scotland routing
  • Premium small-group and multi-day touring

Controls & performance

Operational performance metrics

Touring stability is protected through feasibility checks, driver-hours awareness and structured escalation during live movements. The metrics below describe the operational intent used for programme delivery and internal review.

Delivery targets

  • On-time departure intent: 98%+ (programme dependent)
  • Structured itinerary feasibility review before confirmation
  • Defined vehicle specification and service scope
  • Live communication protocols during operating days

Compliance principles

  • Zero tolerance for driver-hours and WTD breaches
  • Tachograph controls and monitoring approach
  • Escalation contact during programme operations
  • Capacity continuity planning during peak periods
Regulatory

Regulatory & licensing overview

We operate with a compliance-first approach aligned to UK operating requirements. Programme delivery is planned to remain feasible within regulatory limits and local access constraints.

Operational requirements

  • Drivers’ Hours and Working Time compliance planning
  • Insurance and risk controls available for review

Programme protection

  • Feasibility-first routing review
  • Hotel and attraction access considerations
  • Contingency awareness for high-density touring days
  • Defined inclusions and exclusions at quote stage
